Tomato Cultivation: Complete Technical Itinerary

Last updated: April 14, 2026

Introduction

Tomatoes are one of the most popular crops in market gardening. Whether you're growing them in a greenhouse or in the open field, following a technical itinerary is essential to maximize yields and prevent diseases. This guide covers everything from sowing to harvest.

Step 1: Sowing

When to Sow

Tomato seeds should be sown indoors 6–8 weeks before the last frost date. In France, this is typically mid-March to early April for outdoor planting in May.

How to Sow

Use a seed-starting mix and plant seeds at a depth of 0.5 cm. Keep the soil temperature between 20–25°C for optimal germination. Seeds usually germinate in 7–14 days.

Transplanting

Once seedlings have 2–3 true leaves, transplant them into individual pots. Harden off the seedlings by gradually exposing them to outdoor conditions 1 week before transplanting.

Step 2: Irrigation

Tomatoes need consistent moisture, but avoid wetting the leaves to prevent diseases like blight. Use drip irrigation for best results. Water 2–3 times per week, depending on weather conditions.

Step 3: Pruning and Training

Prune suckers (side shoots) that grow in the leaf axils to direct energy toward fruit production. Use stakes or trellises to support plants and keep them off the ground.

Step 4: Disease and Pest Management

Common tomato diseases include early blight, late blight, and powdery mildew. Use preventative measures like crop rotation, proper spacing, and resistant varieties. For pests like aphids or whiteflies, use organic pesticides or introduce beneficial insects.

Step 5: Harvesting

Tomatoes are ready to harvest when they are fully colored and slightly soft to the touch. Pick them in the morning for the best flavor. Store at room temperature (not in the fridge).
